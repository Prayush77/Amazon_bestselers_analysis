# Why Cheap Books Rate Just As Well As Expensive Ones.
## Overview
For this  used "Amazon's Top 50 Bestselling Books" dataset from kaggle,which covered all books that make it to that list from 2009-2019 i.e a decade.I was most curious about does price affect the way people rate a book?

## Key Findings
- I found out that price and rating has almost no relationship (correlation: -0.13).
- I was surprised that budget books are rated as high as premium ones.i found out the average rating of Budget and premium are almost same i.e 4.6 .
- Author Dav Pilkey proves that reader loyalty drives consistent success. With 7 fiction books in the dataset, he maintains a stellar 4.9 rating at a lower price point, making him the highest-rated repeat author.
## Surprising Discovery
while going through the data set i found out that a single $105 academic textbook inflated an entire year's average price by nearly $5, teaching me to use .describe() instead of relying on .mean() alone.
